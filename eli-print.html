Pre-Practice — Monday (Footwork + Plyometrics Focus)

⚠️ Confined space — all exercises fit in a 5×10 yard area. No kicking at distance, no goal required. One ball, 4–6 cones, your body.
⏱ Week 1: 20 min total (deliberately light) · Progress to 25–30 min by Week 3
Block 1 — FIFA 11+ Shoulder Warm-Up · 5 min · Every session — never skip
ExerciseSets / RepsWhat & Cue
I / Y / T Positions 10 each position
WHAT = Lie face-down. Arms in I (overhead), Y (45° out), T (straight to sides). Hold 2 sec, squeeze shoulder blades.
💡 Thumbs point up in Y and T. Feel the back of your shoulder, not your neck
Shoulder Tap Push-Ups 2 × 8
WHAT = Push-up, then at the top lift one hand to touch the opposite shoulder. Alternate sides. Hips stay square.
💡 Goal: zero hip rotation when you lift the hand. Widen foot stance for stability
Diagonal Arm Swings 30 sec each arm
WHAT = Swing one arm diagonally — low across body (hip height) to high and out (shoulder height). Controlled, increasing arc over 30 sec.
💡 Let arm weight do the work. Not a stretch — shoulder mobility prep
Block 2 — Footwork & Set Position · 5 min
📐 Target stance width: ~25 in (~64 cm) — 75% of leg length. Wider than natural — that's the point. Adds ~17 in of goal coverage (Ibrahim et al., 2019).
ExerciseSets / RepsWhat & Cue
Wide Stance Drill 2 min
WHAT = Mark ~25 in with two cones. Set feet on cones, drop into athletic position. Hold 3 sec, shuffle 3 steps, re-set. Alternate directions.
💡 The stance will feel too wide. That's correct. Your lower CoG at 5'7" makes this width more powerful for you
Shuffle-Set-Shuffle 2 × 6 reps
WHAT = 3 cones in a line, ~2 yards apart. Shuffle to each cone, achieve correct set position before moving on. 1 rep = touch all 3 cones with a set at each.
💡 The set is the point, not the shuffle. Slow down to hold the position properly
Split Step Timing Drill 10 reps
WHAT = Place ball 1 yard to side. Count "1-2-NOW" — on NOW, perform a small split step hop landing wide, immediately dive laterally to touch ball. Alternate sides.
💡 Dive should initiate DURING the landing of the split step — not after. This is 3–4% faster off the line (Fujimoto et al., 2023)
Block 3 — GK Plyometrics — Lateral Power · 8 min
⏱ Week 1–3: 60–80 foot contacts total · Week 4+: progress to 100–120 · Full rest between sets · Stick every landing 1 second
ExerciseSets × RepsWhat & Cue
Lateral Bounds / Skater Jumps 3 × 5 each side
WHAT = Push off one leg explosively to the side, land on opposite leg only. Stick landing 1 full second. Drive from hip, not knee.
💡 Most GK-specific exercise — mirrors lateral dive power. Control first, power later
Countermovement Jumps (CMJ) 3 × 4
WHAT = Quick hip dip, then explode to max height. Arms drive up. Land softly through ankles/knees/hips. Full reset between reps.
💡 The dip should be fast and shallow — not a slow squat. Speed of the dip loads elastic energy
Pogo Jumps 2 × 10
WHAT = Rapid bounces on both feet — minimal knee bend, movement from ankle only. Stiff, springy legs. Heels never touch down.
💡 Trains ankle stiffness for split step reactivity. Stay on balls of feet entire set
Block 4 — Collapse Dive Progression · 7 min
📌 Key cue every rep: "Ball contacts ground first — not hip, not elbow." Bottom hand behind/under ball, top hand seals. Needs ~3 yards of soft grass.
ExerciseRepsWhat & Cue
Kneeling Collapse 8 each side
WHAT = On knees, ball 1 yard to side. Sweep near-side knee out, transfer weight laterally, fall sideways — bottom hand behind ball first, top hand seals. Alternate sides.
💡 Sweep of the near leg starts the movement. "Scoop and seal." Bottom hand saves, top hand seals
Crouching Collapse 8 each side
WHAT = Same from squat position. Contralateral leg provides push-off force — don't just fall, push laterally. Same hand sequence.
💡 Feel weight shift over near leg as you initiate. The far leg pushes — don't collapse straight down
Standing Collapse 8 each side
WHAT = Full set position. Self-roll ball gently to side (~1.5 yards). Inside step (short step toward ball) first to load muscles, then lateral drive. Bottom hand saves, top seals, roll through hip.
💡 Inside step feels counterintuitive — you step slightly toward ball before driving away from it. This loads faster than stepping outside first
Block 5 — Core & Injury Prevention · 5 min
ExerciseReps / DurationWhat & Cue
Wrist Curls & Finger Extensions 2 min
WHAT = Wrist curls (palm up), reverse curls (palm down), finger spreads held 3 sec, ball squeezes (5 sec). Slow and controlled.
💡 2-minute investment prevents chronic wrist soreness that appears in Weeks 4–6 of high-frequency diving
90/90 Hip Switches 10 slow reps
WHAT = Sit on ground, both knees bent at 90°. Slowly rotate hips to switch sides. Use hands lightly for support. Full range of motion.
💡 Hip external rotators power your lateral dives. Keeping them mobile makes Block 3 more effective
Lateral Leg Swings 10 each leg
WHAT = Stand on one foot, hold cone/wall for balance. Swing free leg across body then out to side. Controlled full range. 10 each leg.
💡 By rep 8–10 feel the end of your hip's range. Never force past comfort
✅ Monday checklist: FIFA 11+ done · Stance width at ~25 in on every shuffle · Ball contacted ground before hip on every dive · No wrist/hip/shoulder pain · Ready for team practice

Pre-Practice — Wednesday (Dive Mechanics + Core Focus)

⚠️ Confined space — same 5×10 yard sideline as Monday. Different exercise emphasis to avoid repeating the identical session.
Block 1 — FIFA 11+ Shoulder Warm-Up · 5 min · Every session — never skip
ExerciseSets / RepsWhat & Cue
I-Position Raise 10 reps
WHAT = Stand, arms at sides. Raise both arms straight in front to shoulder height (thumbs up), hold 1 second at top, lower slowly. This is the I-shape.
💡 Squeeze shoulder blades slightly as you raise. Warms up the posterior rotator cuff — exactly what takes the load when you dive and land on one arm.
Y-Position Raise 10 reps
WHAT = Same starting position. Raise arms to a Y shape — 45° out from straight ahead, thumbs up, to shoulder height. Hold 1 sec, lower slowly.
💡 Keep your chest up — don't let your upper back round forward. The Y hits the lower trapezius, which stabilizes your shoulder during full-extension dives.
T-Position Raise 10 reps
WHAT = Raise arms straight out to the sides — pure T shape, thumbs up, to shoulder height. Hold 1 sec, lower slowly.
💡 Thumbs up, not down. Thumbs-down internally rotates the shoulder and reduces subacromial space — that's how you get impingement over a season.
Push-Up with Plus 8 reps
WHAT = Standard push-up. At the top of each rep, push your shoulder blades apart an extra inch — "plus" them forward. Pause 1 sec, then lower.
💡 The "plus" fires the serratus anterior — a GK's shoulder armor. It keeps your scapula against your ribs during impact. Don't skip the plus.
Diagonal Arm Pattern 8 each side
WHAT = Standing. Sweep one arm from low across your body (near opposite hip) diagonally up to high reach on the same side — like drawing a sword overhead. Slow and controlled. 8 reps each arm.
💡 Mimics the full-extension dive reach pattern. Feel the stretch across your chest and lat at the top. Move with control — this is prep, not speed.
Block 2 — Ball Handling & Reaction · 5 min
ExerciseRepsWhat & Cue
W-Shape Hand Drill 15 catches
WHAT = Self-toss to chest height. Catch with thumbs together pointing toward each other behind ball (W shape), fingers spread wide. Soft hands — don't grab, let ball come to you.
💡 If the ball stings, thumbs are too far apart. Soft absorption, not a catch-and-clench
Quick Hands Ground Pick-Up 10 each side
WHAT = Ball 1 yard to side. Drop into low scoop — bottom hand behind/under, top hand seals — in one smooth motion. Return to set. Alternate sides.
💡 Lead with bottom hand first — it travels furthest. Top hand just needs to seal. "Reach under, seal above"
Reaction Catch 10 each variation
WHAT = Var A: Hold ball at arm's length, drop it, catch before 2nd bounce. Var B: Hold behind back over shoulder, drop to side, spin and catch before 2nd bounce.
💡 Don't anticipate the bounce — that cheats the drill. Your first reaction to an unpredictable event is what this trains
Block 3 — GK Plyometrics — Explosion & Reaction · 8 min
Different exercises from Monday — single-leg stability and vertical emphasis. Weeks 1–3: skip Tuck Jumps, do extra High Ball Claims instead.
ExerciseSets × RepsWhat & Cue
Single-Leg Lateral Hops 2 × 4 each side
WHAT = Balance on one foot. Hop laterally over a line (crack or cone) 4 times. Control each landing — 1 sec stick. Trains exact muscle pattern of a lateral dive landing.
💡 Wobbling on landing = that leg is weak. Note which side and tell your GK coach
High Ball Claim (Vertical) 10 reps
WHAT = Self-toss 2–3 ft above head. Jump and catch at highest point — W hands, pull firmly to chest, land on both feet. Confined-space vertical claim drill — no running approach needed.
💡 Claim at PEAK of jump, not on the way down. Yell "keeper!" on each rep — train the command too
Tuck Jumps (Phase 2+ only — Week 4+) 2 × 4
WHAT = Explosive vertical jump, drive both knees toward chest at peak. Land softly. Full reset between reps. Weeks 1–3: skip this, add extra High Ball Claims instead.
💡 Week 4: introduce at 50% effort. Week 5: build to full effort. Highest-impact movement in the pre-practice program
Block 4 — 1v1 Body Shape & Positioning · 7 min
📊 Wear et al. (2022) — 323 EPL/World Cup 1v1s: staying back and reacting is the LEAST effective strategy. Spread/K-block at close range is 10% more likely to result in a save.
ExerciseRepsWhat & Cue
K-Block / Spread Position 3 reps × 3 distances
WHAT = 2 cones as simulated goal (~3 yds apart). At 1, 2, and 3 yards in front: drop into K-block/spread — wide legs, low CoG, arms spread as wide as possible. Hold 2 sec. 9 total reps.
💡 Arms out and LOW — not high. Make your body as wide as the goal. "Become the obstacle"
Aggressive Set — Closing Position 10 walk-in reps
WHAT = Start 8 yards from cones. Walk forward assertively while maintaining athletic posture. At 4–5 yards, plant and shift into spread/K-block. Practice the transition — movement to set.
💡 Set feet the moment the attacker regains control. Not before, not after. Precise timing is the skill
Diamond Footwork 2 sets
WHAT = 4 cones in diamond shape (~3 yards between points). Shuffle forward → lateral → backward → lateral. Set position at each cone. One full loop = 1 rep.
💡 Matches GK movement when adjusting to a cross or ball moving across the box. Every cone = a new incoming threat
Block 5 — Core & Injury Prevention · 5 min
ExerciseReps / DurationWhat & Cue
Wrist Curls & Finger Extensions 2 min
WHAT = Wrist curls (palm up), reverse curls (palm down), finger spreads held 3 sec, ball squeezes (5 sec). Slow and controlled. If wrists feel sore from Monday's diving, focus only on finger extensions and ball squeezes — skip curls.
💡 Listen to your body — soreness from Monday's diving means the tissue is adapting. Reduce curls if tender. 2-minute investment prevents chronic wrist soreness that appears in Weeks 4–6 of high-frequency diving.
Hip Flexor Stretch 30 sec each side
WHAT = Kneeling lunge — front foot flat, back knee on ground. Drive hips forward until stretch at front of back hip. Hold 30 sec. Switch.
💡 Keep torso upright. Stretch is at the FRONT of the back hip. If you feel it in the knee, adjust foot position
Glute Bridge Hold 3 × 10 sec hold
WHAT = Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat. Drive hips up until body forms a straight line shoulder to knee. Squeeze glutes hard. Hold 10 sec. Lower slowly.
💡 Push knees slightly apart at the top (hip abduction). Hold without breathing for the last 3 seconds of each rep
✅ Wednesday checklist: FIFA 11+ done · W-shape hand position clean · 1v1 body positions at all 3 distances · No wrist/hip/shoulder pain · Ready for team practice

Weekend Session — Open Field Required (45–60 min)

🟢 Open field required: 30+ yards clear, park goals acceptable, room to kick and retrieve. No confined-space exercises in this session.
📌 Priority order: Goal Kicks → Distribution → Partner or Solo block. If only 25 min available, do Goal Kicks + Distribution only.
Goal Kick Block — 15 min · 15–20 kicks · ALWAYS FIRST (your #1 gap)
⚠️ Technique sequence: Week 1 = ankle lock only · Week 2 = add plant foot · Week 3 = add approach angle · Week 4+ = integration. One element per week.
ExerciseRepsWhat & Cue
Ankle Lock Isolation (Also OK in confined space) 15 reps
WHAT = Stand in place, hold ball at waist height. Drop onto planted instep — ankle rigidly locked (fully plantarflexed, toes pointed). Ball bounces cleanly upward. Ball squirting sideways = ankle was loose.
💡 Ankle lock is almost certainly your primary failure point. Punt uses a forgiving swing; goal kick demands a rigid "brick" foot at contact
Plant Foot Placement Drill 10 reps
WHAT = Mark plant foot position with a coin: ~2–4 in beside ball, level with it, toes toward target. Full approach and plant step — hit the marker — WITHOUT kicking. Check position, walk away, reset.
💡 Plant foot toes point AT your target. Not sideways, not inward. Plant beside and level — not behind — the ball
Short-Distance Goal Kicks (15–20 yds) 10–15 kicks
WHAT = Ball on ground, 6-yard box. Approach 30–45° from kick direction. Lock ankle, plant correctly, strike instep just below ball's equatorial midline. Target cones at 15–20 yds. Focus: get ball airborne with correct trajectory — not max distance.
💡 Strike just BELOW the equatorial midline — not completely underneath. Feel the proximal-to-distal chain: pelvis → hip → thigh → shank → locked foot
Progressive Distance Kicks (Week 4+) 10–15 kicks
WHAT = Add 5 yards per week once technique is clean. Wk 4: 20–25 yds. Wk 6: 25–35 yds. Wk 8+: full-field goal kicks. Follow-through lands several steps ahead of ball.
💡 Timeline: Weeks 1–3 = contact quality · Weeks 4–6 = height and accuracy · Week 8+ = distance gains of 5–10 yards
Distribution Block — 10 min · Open Field
ExerciseRepsWhat & Cue
Overarm Throw Accuracy 5 throws × 3 targets
WHAT = 3 cones at 15, 20, 25 yards. 5 throws to each. Track how many land within 1 yard of the cone. Note your "reliable throw distance."
💡 Your reliable throw distance expands with deliberate practice. Track the within-1-yard number each week
Driven Pass to Target 10 each foot
WHAT = Ground driven passes (instep) to cone at 20–30 yards. Pace that a teammate can receive in stride. Dominant foot, then non-dominant.
💡 Accuracy beats power. A ball that hits the target at 25 yards is more useful than a missile that sails past
Punt Maintenance 10 punts
WHAT = 10 punts to target zone at 35–40 yards. Placement and trajectory consistency — not max distance. Punts are already a strength — this is maintenance only. Do NOT over-train what's working.
💡 If one or two felt off, take 2–3 more to identify what changed (approach angle? drop height? contact point?). Then stop
🤝 Partner Block — 20–30 min (When Dad / Partner is Available)
🤝 Use this block when Dad is available. Focuses on scenarios that REQUIRE a live server: served extension dives, reaction saves, 1v1 scenarios.
ExerciseRepsWhat & Cue
Power Step Dive from Knees (Served) 6 each side
WHAT = Start kneeling. Partner serves a ball just beyond collapse range. Load near-side leg, explode laterally to catch before ball lands. Full rest between reps.
💡 Near foot loads, then push explosively. Contralateral leg is already airborne — it provides the "tail" of the dive. Ball first, landing second
Standing Power Step Dive (Served) 6 each side
WHAT = Full standing set. Partner serves beyond collapse range — top or bottom corner, varying. Power step and full extension. You call ready, Dad serves.
💡 Your lower CoG (5'7") is an advantage for low extension dives — cover bottom corners more efficiently than a taller keeper. Emphasize low-corner dives
Reaction Save — Mixed Height/Distance 10–12 serves
WHAT = Dad 8–10 yards away with several balls. Eli in set position. Dad serves (thrown, rolled, or tossed) to any location without telegraphing. Eli reacts and saves. No pattern, no preparation.
💡 Watch the server's hands and shoulders for earliest cue — not just the ball. Train the habit of reading body language before the shot
Distance Recognition Drill (Phase 2+) 10 reps
WHAT = Dad dribbles slowly from 15 yards in. Eli calls technique verbally: "SET" (beyond 8 yds), "CLOSING" (8–5 yds), "SPREAD" (under 5 yds central). No actual diving — decision practice only.
💡 Call technique BEFORE Dad reaches the next zone. Hesitation is the real opponent. Watch the kicking foot specifically
Live 1v1 — Full Speed (Phase 2+ only) 6–8 reps
WHAT = Dad receives at 12 yards, takes one touch, attacks at realistic speed. Eli must read, close if appropriate, set feet when Dad regains control, use correct technique by distance. Full rest between reps.
💡 Framework: 1) Can I win the ball? 2) Is the touch loose? 3) Set feet when he regains control. 4) React by distance. Stay on feet longer — don't dive early
✅ After partner block: hip flexor stretch (30 sec each), quad/glute stretch (30 sec each), wrist circles (1 min).
🧍 Solo Block — 20 min (Use When Training Alone — Swap for Partner Block Above)
🧍 Use this block when Dad is NOT available. Goal kicks and distribution are already done — this block keeps momentum with self-serve options.
ExerciseRepsWhat & Cue
Self-Serve Extension Dive 6 each side
WHAT = Self-toss ball up and to the side, slightly beyond collapse range. Execute full power step dive to catch before second bounce. Gradually increase toss distance over the session.
💡 You're the server AND the keeper. Make the toss challenging but not impossible. Full extension — fingertips touch ball first
Additional Goal Kicks 10 more kicks
WHAT = 10 additional goal kicks — groove reps at current technique focus (ankle lock / plant foot / approach angle). Lower intensity than the primary block. Reinforce not strain.
💡 Every kick gives immediate feedback: clean flight or squirt sideways. Track the ratio mentally
1v1 Body Shape Review 3 positions × 3 distances
WHAT = Set 2 cones as goal. Practice K-block, passive set, and aggressive set at 3 distances. Hold each 2 seconds. 9 total reps. Reinforce positions built in pre-practice.
💡 Open field makes these positions feel realistic — visualize an actual shooter approaching
Reaction Catch — Behind-the-Back Advanced 10 each variation
WHAT = Advanced version: drop ball behind back at increasing height, add quarter-turn before catching. Free-form reaction training — experiment with variations. Both sides.
💡 The more unpredictable your own drill is, the better your reflexes train. Solo training is an opportunity to experiment
✅ After solo block: hip flexor stretch (30 sec each), quad/glute stretch (30 sec each), wrist circles (1 min).

8-Week Periodization Plan

📋 At the start of each week, check your phase. Apply volume and intensity guidance. Use the Coach Communication note to brief your Thursday GK coach.
Phase 1 — Foundation Weeks 1–3 · RPE 5–6
Build the technical foundation. Learn what correct feels like before adding intensity. The first 3 weeks should feel manageable — that's by design.
Pre-PracticeWeek 1: 20 min deliberately light. Progress to 25–30 min by Week 3. Diving: kneeling → crouching → standing collapse only. No power step dives yet. Plyometrics: 60–80 foot contacts Mon + Wed. Skip tuck jumps.
WeekendGoal kicks: Wk 1 = ankle lock isolation only. Wk 2 = add plant foot drill. Wk 3 = add short-distance kicks at 15–20 yds. No live 1v1 yet — body shape practice only.
Baseline TestEnd of Week 1 weekend session: measure lateral dive reach, standing broad jump, T-test time, goal kick distance (avg 5 kicks). Log in Goals section.
Coach Note"I'm working on wider set position (~25 in width) and collapse dive mechanics. Can we reinforce set position width and low-ball technique in Thursday sessions?"
Week 1 is a baseline and habit-formation week — not a performance week. If pre-practice feels easy, that's correct.
Phase 2 — Development Weeks 4–6 · RPE 7–8
Add intensity and complexity. Live 1v1 introduced on partner days. Plyometric volume peaks. Goal kick distance begins to progress. This is the hardest block.
Pre-PracticeFull 25–30 min sessions. Introduce tuck jumps (Wednesday). Plyometrics: 100–120 foot contacts across Mon + Wed. Add reaction elements to footwork. Introduce power step component to diving.
WeekendGoal kicks progressing to 20–25 yards. Introduce live 1v1 (Distance Recognition + Live Scenario) with partner. Extension diving with served balls. Reaction saves with increasing unpredictability.
Re-TestEnd of Week 4: lateral dive reach, T-test time, goal kick distance. Compare to Week 1 baseline.
Coach Note"I'm working on 1v1 decision-making — passive set beyond 8 yards, aggressive set at 8–5 yards, spread inside 5 yards. Can we include breakaway scenarios in Thursday sessions?"
If heavy/sore going into Thursday double-session: cut Wednesday pre-practice to 10-min mobility only. Protect the coaching session.
Phase 3 — Sharpening Weeks 7–8 · RPE 8, Volume –20–30%
Maintain intensity but reduce volume. Start feeling sharp, not tired. The training is complete — now maintaining the edge.
Pre-Practice20 min sessions. Cut 1 set from each plyometric exercise. Maintain collapse dive — skip kneeling stage, go straight to crouching and standing.
WeekendConfidence-building drills with high success rate. Goal kicks at current best distance (not pushing further). Partner: reaction saves and 1v1 feel — no new techniques.
Re-TestEnd of Week 7: final comparison — lateral dive reach, T-test, goal kick distance. This is your tryout-readiness snapshot.
Coach Note"Tryouts are in 2 weeks. Can we simulate tryout-format drills — shot-stopping sequences, 1v1 breakaways, distribution under pressure?"
Weeks 7–8: feeling sharp and confident IS the goal. Reduce load before you feel tired, not after.
Phase 4 — Taper Final 7–10 Days · 100% freq · 90% intensity · 50% volume
The 100:90:50 rule. Feel fresh and sharp, not flat from rest. Trust the preparation.
Pre-Practice15 min only — FIFA 11+ shoulder + footwork + light plyometrics. NO diving in the final 5 days.
PlyometricsLast heavy session: 7–10 days before tryout. After: light pogo jumps and CMJs only to stay springy. No new exercises.
DivingLast heavy diving session: 5–7 days before tryout (coordinate with GK coach). After: hands-only catching and light footwork.
WeekendWeekend before tryout: 20–30 min goal kicks (feel-good reps, not max effort) + light distribution. NO new techniques. Familiarity only.
Final 48 hrsLight ball handling, visualization, mobility only. No training. Replay your best saves, goal kicks, and 1v1 wins mentally.
The training is done. The adaptations are in your muscles and decision-making patterns. Tryout day is when you display what's already there. Trust it.
