Friday Session — Full Development Session (45–60 min)

✅ No game today. No practice before this. Full weekend ahead to recover. This is the primary development window of the week.
Block 1 — Dynamic Warm-Up · 5 min
ExerciseDurationWhat & Cue
Jog + High Knees + Butt Kicks 2 min
WHAT = Easy jog 20 yds. High knees back (knee to hip, quick arms). Butt kicks there. Repeat once. Gradual effort increase.
💡 Neural activation — quick rhythmic reps, not slow deliberate ones. Wakes up fast-twitch before you ask it to perform
A-Skips + B-Skips 2 lengths each
WHAT = A-Skip: march/skip with high knee to 90°, push ball of opposite foot into ground. B-Skip: same but extend lower leg forward at peak, punch it down. ~20 yds each length.
💡 A-skip = knee drive. B-skip = pawback. Sprint mechanics done slowly — carry over directly to the acceleration block
Hip Flexor Lunge Walk + Rotation 1 min
WHAT = Walking lunges — deep step forward, hold 1 sec at bottom, rotate torso toward front knee, stand up, step other foot forward. 10 yds forward and back.
💡 Opens hip flexors of the trailing (push-off) leg. The most common limiter of acceleration range at 13
Block 2 — Ball Mastery Circuit · 10 min · Never shorten this block
Quality first, speed second. Eyes up after the first 30 sec of each exercise. If time is short on a given Friday, trim the speed block — never this one.
ExerciseDurationWhat & Cue
Sole Roll Figure-8 2 min
WHAT = 2 cones 3 yds apart. Sole-roll ball in figure-8 around both, alternating feet on each half. Continuous, increasing speed. Eyes up after 1 min.
💡 Shielding + first-touch skill. Ball mastery that only works while staring at the ball isn't game-ready
Inside-Outside Chop Zigzag 2 min
WHAT = 4 cones 2 yds apart. Inside chop at one cone, outside chop at next. Both feet. Last 30 sec: outside chop only.
💡 Outside chop creates the most space in 1v1s — defenders don't expect it. Over-emphasize every session
L-Turn Combination 10 each foot
WHAT = Approach cone at jog. Plant inside foot beside ball. Sole of other foot stops ball. Drag back. Push 90° with inside of plant foot. Burst 3 yds. Alternate exit direction each rep.
💡 Every rep ends with a 3-yd burst — not a jog. The acceleration after the turn is the whole point
Cruyff Turn 8 each foot
WHAT = Approach cone as if crossing. Wind up/fake the action. Drag ball behind standing foot with inside of shooting foot. Pivot 180°. Push into space with other foot. Accelerate. 8 reps dragging foot per side.
💡 Exaggerate the fake every rep. No convincing fake = no space from the turn. Build this habit even in solo practice
Scissors / Step-Over 8 each foot
WHAT = Approach cone at jog. Step one foot over ball in circular arc (inside to outside). Push ball away with outside of other foot. Accelerate. Once clean: try double-scissors before push.
💡 Commit your weight toward the fake foot before pushing away — without that weight shift the move doesn't sell
Block 3 — Weak Foot Development · 10 min · Non-dominant foot only · All 10 min
Non-dominant foot ONLY for all exercises. Never switch to strong foot when it gets hard. Record clean contact ratio every Friday — it's your primary weak foot data point.
ExerciseDurationWhat & Cue
Wall Pass — Weak Foot Only 3 min
WHAT = 4 yds from wall. Continuous weak foot passes — control return, reset, pass. Min 1: hit wall. Min 2: hit a target spot. Min 3: increase pace, one-touch if possible.
💡 First 20 passes feel wrong. By rep 40 it clicks. Each week the click comes sooner
Receive + Redirect — Weak Foot 20 reps
WHAT = Roll ball 2 yds ahead. Control with weak foot inside — redirect 45° into space. Push 1 yd that direction, jog after, repeat other direction. 20 total. Week 3+: receive from wall rebound.
💡 First touch should put ball where you want to go next — not just in front of you. That 45° habit is the whole drill
Weak Foot Drive · ⏱ Record ratio 10 strikes
WHAT = Ball stationary 10 yds from wall/cone. Gentle angle approach. Drive with laces, weak foot only. Count clean (straight line, pace) vs mishit (spin, scoop, veer). Record x/10 clean every Friday.
💡 Track this number weekly. Watching 4/10 become 8/10 over 8 weeks is the best motivation you'll find
Weak Foot L-Turn + Push 8 reps
WHAT = L-turn mechanics but weak foot does the dragging and the pushing. Sole-drag back with weak foot, push 90° with weak foot inside surface, burst. 8 reps, alternate exit direction.
💡 When you can execute the L-turn with either foot, you can receive and turn in any direction — that's genuine two-footedness in a game-realistic sense
Block 4 — Speed + Footwork · 15 min · TIME yourself every Friday
Full rest between every sprint rep — quality at 95–100% beats volume at 80%.
ExerciseRepsWhat & Cue
T-Drill ⏱ 5 timed · record best
WHAT = Start → top cone 10 yds → shuffle left 5 yds (touch) → shuffle right 10 yds (touch) → shuffle left to center → backpedal 10 yds start. 5 reps, 30 sec rest. Record best.
💡 Baseline age 13: 11–12 sec. Under 10.5 = excellent. Goal: drop 0.5–1 sec over 8 weeks
5-10-5 Pro Agility ⏱ 5 timed · record best
WHAT = Center → sprint 5 yds right (touch) → sprint 10 yds left (touch) → sprint 5 yds back through center. 5 reps, alternate start direction, 30 sec rest. Record best.
💡 Drive LOW into cuts — hips drop, not rise. A good cut sounds sharp on turf, not a skid
Falling Starts 6 × 10 yds
WHAT = Stand tall, fall forward past tipping point, catch with explosive first step. Drive arms hard. 10 yds full effort. Walk back. 6 reps, 30 sec rest. Alternate catching foot each rep.
💡 Forces correct forward lean before foot leaves the ground. Most players start too upright — this wires the correct pattern automatically
Reaction Starts — Visual Cue 6 × 10 yds
WHAT = Athletic position. Drop phone/ball from waist — sprint 10 yds the instant it hits the ground. Vary starting position each rep: forward, sideways, facing away. 20 sec rest.
💡 Facing-away reps train the pivot-open-accelerate sequence — the most common game sprint direction change
Quick Feet Cone Touch ⏱ 3 × 10 sec · count touches
WHAT = 5 cones 1 yd apart. Alternate toe-touches as fast as possible. 10 sec on, 15 sec off. 3 sets. Count touches/set. Record best.
💡 Stay on toes entire time. Arms drive to match feet — often the cue that unlocks a faster set
Box Drill — With Ball 3 each direction
WHAT = 4 cones in 5-yd square. Sprint, shuffle, backpedal, shuffle — ball at feet throughout every movement. 3 clockwise, 3 counter-clockwise.
💡 Tight control = faster movement. This transfers more to game situations than any other agility drill in the plan
Block 5 — Strength + Shielding · 5 min
ExerciseDurationWhat & Cue
Shield Stance Holds 5 × 10 sec
WHAT = Ball pinned under sole. Wide low stance: feet shoulder-width+, knees ~90°, chest forward, arms out. Hold 10 sec. 5 holds, alternate feet.
💡 If falling easily, weight is too high — drop hips. Even weight distribution across both feet
Push-Up Max Reps · ⏱ record 2 × to failure
WHAT = Standard push-ups to failure — chest to ground, body straight, elbows 45° from body. 2 sets, 30 sec rest. Record max reps every Friday.
💡 Every 5 extra push-ups = noticeably harder shoulder barge in games. Track this number weekly
Single-Leg Squat Hold 5 × 10 sec each leg
WHAT = Stand on one leg, lower into quarter squat. Hold 10 sec, knee tracking over toes (not caving inward). 5 holds each leg.
💡 Knee caving inward = hip abductor weakness showing up. This is also an injury risk indicator — flag it if persistent
✅ Friday checklist: Warm-up w/ A/B skips · Ball mastery Cruyff fake convincing · Eyes up on figure-8 after 1 min · Weak foot 3 min unbroken · Clean contacts: __/10 · T-test: __ sec · Pro Agility: __ sec · Quick feet: __ / 10 sec · Push-up max: __ reps · Hip flexors + hamstrings stretched

Weekend Session — Open Field (45–60 min)

🟠 Syncs with Eli's session. First 25 min are parallel independent blocks. Last 20–25 min are shared partner training.
📌 Order: Sprint Development (parallel w/ Eli goal kicks) → Weak Foot Shooting (parallel w/ Eli distribution) → Partner Block (shared) → Cool-down.
Block 1 — Sprint Development · 15 min · Parallel with Eli's Goal Kick Block
Full rest between sprint reps. Quality at 95–100% beats volume at 80%.
ExerciseRepsWhat & Cue
Falling Start Accelerations 5 × 10 yds
WHAT = Fall-and-catch start, arms drive, 10 yds full effort. Full walk-back rest. 5 reps. Reps 4–5 should feel faster than rep 1.
💡 If later reps feel worse — rest periods too short
Acceleration Builds — 20 yds 4 × 20 yds
WHAT = First 7 yds = 60%, next 7 yds = 85%, last 6 yds = 100%. Full walk rest. Trains ability to keep accelerating past the initial burst.
💡 Still accelerating at yard 20 = correct. Just holding from yard 10 = not training the curve
Flying 10s ⏱ · record fastest 3 timed reps
WHAT = 10-yd jog run-up to reach full stride. Sprint absolute hardest for the next 10 yds. Time only the flying segment. Full rest. 3 reps. Record fastest.
💡 True top-end speed measurement — already at full stride when the clock starts. Track every weekend
Ball Sprint Integration 4 reps (2 strong / 2 weak)
WHAT = Dribble 70% for 10 yds. Push ball 3 yds ahead (knock past a defender). Burst 100% to recover. 2 reps dominant push, 2 reps weak foot push.
💡 Weak foot push reps are the important ones — when that's reliable you're genuinely two-footed and unpredictable
Block 2 — Weak Foot Shooting · 10 min · Parallel with Eli's Distribution Block · At real goal
ExerciseRepsWhat & Cue
Stationary Weak Foot Drives · ⏱ record on-target 10 shots · 12 yds
WHAT = Stationary ball, 12 yds from goal, 30° approach. Drive into bottom corners — 5 shots bottom-left, 5 shots bottom-right. Record on-target count every weekend.
💡 Bottom corners are hardest for keepers to save. Aim there, not at the middle
Inside-Foot Placement — Weak Foot 10 shots · 10 yds
WHAT = Inside surface of weak foot only. 5 shots near post, 5 far post. 10 yds out. Accuracy over power.
💡 Build accuracy first. A well-placed side-foot far post beats a powerful mishit to the middle
Moving Weak Foot Finish 10 shots
WHAT = Dribble from 20 yds, cut to weak foot side, shoot on the move. Shoot from wherever you arrive. 10 reps, different approach angle each time.
💡 Most game-realistic drill in the block. Friday builds mechanics; weekend reps build application
🤝 Block 3 — Eli + Isaiah Partner Session · 20–25 min
🤝 Requires both players. Substitute Solo Finishing Block if Eli unavailable. Both players get genuine training value — mutual work.
ExerciseRepsRoles + Cue
Isaiah Serves → Eli Extension Dives IsaiahEli 6 each side
WHAT = Isaiah rolls or throws balls just beyond Eli's collapse range. Eli executes power-step dives. Isaiah varies height each serve: rolling, mid-height, shoulder-high. Aim for a specific spot per serve.
💡 Isaiah: serve accuracy = throw-in precision. Eli: watch arm angle, not the ball
1v1 Full Speed — Isaiah Attacks IsaiahEli 8 reps
WHAT = Isaiah starts 12 yds from goal with ball. Eli in goal. Game speed. Full rest between reps. Isaiah must use a different skill move each rep — no pure-pace runs allowed.
💡 Isaiah: rotate Cruyff, L-turn, outside chop, scissors. Eli: call technique name before executing
Eli Rolls → Isaiah Weak Foot Finish EliIsaiah 10 reps × 2 sets
WHAT = Set A: Eli rolls to Isaiah's weak foot side (15 yds). Control on weak foot, one touch, shoot weak foot. Set B: Eli rolls to strong side — Isaiah transfers and finishes weak foot. 10 reps each set.
💡 Set B simulates far-post cross finishing — hardest and most game-valuable weak foot drill in the plan
Eli Punts → Isaiah First Touch + Turn EliIsaiah 8 reps
WHAT = Eli punts high ball 15–20 yds. Isaiah controls with chest/thigh/foot directing it 2–3 yds into space (not dead at feet). Second touch: drive and shoot. Eli varies height each rep.
💡 Isaiah: first touch into space keeps you free; dead at feet lets a defender close. Eli: punt maintenance work
✅ After any block: hip flexor (30 sec each) · hamstring (20 sec each) · quad (20 sec each) · ankle circles

Solo Finishing Block · 20 min — Use when Eli is unavailable

ExerciseRepsWhat & Cue
Combination Finishing 10 reps varied
WHAT = Self-roll ball 5 yds, sprint onto it, one-touch set, finish. Vary: driven, placed, weak foot, outside, on the move. Name finish type before each rep.
💡 Deliberate variety beats random repetition
Skill Move + Finish 10 reps
WHAT = Start 25 yds from goal. Dribble, execute one ball mastery move at cone "defender" 12 yds out, finish. Different move each rep — never same twice in a row.
💡 Commit to every move as if the cone is real. Half-committed practice = half-committed games
Extra Weak Foot Shots 10 shots
WHAT = 5 shots from 10 yds, 5 from 15 yds, weak foot only.
💡 Solo days get extra weak foot volume — a genuine advantage, not a compromise

8-Week Training Structure

📋 No imminent tryout. Phase 4 is a forward maintenance plan. This 8-week block is a template to repeat, not a finish line.
Phase 1 — FoundationWeeks 1–3 · RPE 5–6
Build habits before adding intensity. Learn what correct feels like. Establish baselines.
FridayWeek 1: RPE 5–6. Ball mastery at controlled speed. Speed block: falling starts + box drill only, skip reaction starts. 45 min max. Week 3: all blocks at full structure.
Weak FootWall passes + stationary shots only. Count clean contacts vs mishits every session. Measure honestly — you need a real baseline.
WeekendNo flying 10s — falling starts and 20-yd builds only. Eli 1v1s at 70% speed. Verbalize what GK technique Eli is using so Isaiah can practice reading it.
BaselineEnd of Week 1: T-test, pro agility, flying 10, broad jump, weak foot ratio. Starting numbers — not judgments.
Week 1 should feel manageable. Build the habit before the intensity.
Phase 2 — DevelopmentWeeks 4–6 · RPE 7–8
Push intensity, add complexity. Reaction starts. Flying 10s timed. 1v1s full speed. Weak foot shifts to application.
FridayAll acceleration drills including reaction starts. Ball mastery: add directional constraints to force weak-side decisions. T-test and pro agility should be dropping.
Weak FootMoving weak foot work. Weak foot L-turn introduced. Game target: 3 weak foot passes per match mentally tracked.
WeekendFlying 10s timed. Full-speed 1v1s. Skill move required every 1v1 rep — no pure pace.
Mid-PointEnd of Week 4: re-test all. Agility adaptation shows fastest — clear movement by now.
If Friday leaves heavy legs going into a game week → trim speed block, keep ball mastery and weak foot in full.
Phase 3 — ConsolidationWeeks 7–8 · Volume down, intensity maintained
Trust the patterns. Apply deliberately in games. Final measurements.
FridayCut 1 set from each speed exercise. Full ball mastery + weak foot blocks — never shortened. RPE 8 on remaining reps.
Final TestEnd of Week 7: T-test, pro agility, flying 10, broad jump, weak foot ratio. Compare to Week 1 — this is the evidence.
Game FocusOne intention per match: "only skill moves to beat defenders" or "every weak foot attempt I complete." Single focus compounds.
Habits are set by now. Trust them.
Phase 4 — Maintenance + Next BlockOngoing · Summer + Next Season
Forward plan. 8-week template repeats. Friday sessions continue through summer.
SummerKeep Friday habit — park or field. 2 sessions/week if possible. Partner blocks with Eli or Silas when schedules align. The habit of showing up is the most transferable skill from this program.
Weak FootTarget for fall 8th grade: 8/10 clean contacts. Math: 3 blocks/week × 40 weeks ≈ 12,000 weak foot touches. That's what closes the gap permanently.
Varsity Path7th grade now. Varsity typically 10th (age 15–16). Players who make it at 16 started this quiet work at 13. Most competition will not.
The goal isn't this season — it's having options in 3 years.
