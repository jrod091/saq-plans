⚽ Part 1 — Ball Work · 15 min Forward Drive & Receiving in Stride
🎯 All exercises done solo with rebounder — no partner needed
🦶 Weak Foot First Rule — For every exercise that works both feet, always start with your weak foot and complete all reps before switching to the dominant foot

Foundation — 3 min · Every day

Sole Rolls Duration30 sec each foot
WHAT = Ball under sole, roll it across your body using just the bottom of your foot, alternating feet continuously
💡 Stay on the balls of your feet. Keep the ball close — smooth rhythm, not speed
Inside / Outside Taps Duration30 sec each foot
WHAT = Tap ball with inside of foot, then outside of same foot, alternating, moving slowly forward. All reps on one foot then switch
💡 Soft touch each tap. Ball stays within one foot of you at all times
Toe Taps Duration45 sec
WHAT = Alternate feet tapping the top of the ball rapidly, light and quick
💡 Light quick taps — feet barely leave the ground. Stay bouncy, stay on toes
Slow Scissors Duration10 each foot
WHAT = Step over the ball with one foot, shift your full weight, step over with the other. Full deliberate scissors — not rushed
💡 Commit to the full body feint even going slowly. This is where the move gets grooved into muscle memory

Category Work — Linear

Rebounder 1-Touch Wall Pass Duration45 sec each foot — weak foot first
WHAT = Face rebounder 3–4 yards away. Play 1-touch passes continuously with the inside of the same foot the whole set. WEAK FOOT FIRST — complete the full set on weak foot before switching
💡 Get your foot behind the ball early. Firm pass into the rebounder, relaxed receive. Stay on your toes between each touch
Receive & Drive Duration8 reps
WHAT = Kick to rebounder at a slight angle. The moment you kick, take 1 lateral step away from your starting spot. Receive the rebound while moving, first touch out of your feet in the direction of a cone, drive to the cone, jog back. That's 1 rep
💡 The 1 lateral step is what creates movement on the receive — you're not standing still waiting. That habit transfers directly to receiving in a game
Two-Touch Combination Duration10 reps each side
WHAT = Face rebounder directly. Pass with weak foot, receive with strong foot, pass back with strong foot, receive with weak foot — alternate feet continuously. 10 reps = 10 full exchanges. Then swap which foot leads
💡 Don't let the ball come all the way to a stop between touches — meet it early. This builds the quick two-touch rhythm you use when combining under pressure
Variable-Distance Wall Pass Duration~2 min
WHAT = Place a cone at 5 yards and another at 8 yards from the rebounder. Stand at 5-yard cone, play 6 passes — feel the ball returning at comfortable pace. Walk to 8-yard cone, play 6 passes — feel the extra force needed. Then alternate: 1 pass at 5 yards, walk to 8, 1 pass, walk back. Repeat 5 times
💡 On every rep ask: did the ball arrive at the right height and pace? Too hard, too soft, or just right? The goal is to feel the force difference between distances and make the adjustment automatic — this is what pass weight calibration feels like

Position Work — Winger

Push & Chase Duration8 reps each side
WHAT = Push the ball hard forward at a diagonal angle — as if knocking it into space on the wing — sprint to catch it at full pace, control in stride with a clean first touch, drive to a cone. Walk back. That's 1 rep
💡 Push it further than feels comfortable and chase it at full pace. This trains receiving the ball at speed. If you always push it short, you're jogging to the ball — not receiving in stride like a winger
Drive the Line Duration6 reps each side
WHAT = Receive from rebounder wide, push ball at full speed along a line of cones to the far end, sharp inside cut. Walk back. That's 1 rep
💡 Push the ball further ahead than feels comfortable and chase it — that's how you beat defenders with pace. If the ball is under your feet, you can't accelerate
🏃 Part 2 — SAQ · 15 min
⏱ Timing — 3 passes per drill · Walk back after each pass · 20 sec rest before Pass 3 · ~7.5 min ladder

Ladder · 5 drills

1 LINEAR SL Hop — Std (L) Passes3 Rest~20s
1 PASS = Hop on LEFT foot into EVERY rung, one hop per rung, full length
💡 Ball of foot only, knee soft on landing, arms relaxed at sides
2 LINEAR SL Hop — Std (R) Passes3 Rest~20s
1 PASS = Hop on RIGHT foot into EVERY rung, one hop per rung, full length
💡 Mirror left exactly — same posture, same pace. Note if one side feels weaker
3 LINEAR Bunny Hops Passes3 Rest~20s
1 PASS = Both feet land together inside EVERY rung, full length
💡 Land on balls of feet, minimize ground contact time, stay springy between hops
4 LINEAR High Knees Passes3 Rest~20s
1 PASS = One foot per rung alternating — drive each knee up to hip height with every step, full length
💡 Drive opposite arm to opposite knee hard. Stay on balls of feet. Exaggerate the knee lift — this is a sprint mechanics drill
5 LINEAR Up & Down Passes3 Rest~20s
1 PASS = BOTH feet step into rung 1, BOTH into rung 2, BOTH back into rung 1, then progress. Pattern: forward-forward, back-one. Full length
💡 Two steps forward, one step back — both feet each time. Controlled rhythm. Don't rush the backward step

Resistance Bands

Hip Extension Kick Sets2 Reps10 each leg Rest20s
1 REP = Band around working ankle, anchored in front of you. Stand on the opposite foot. Drive the banded leg straight BACK as far as possible, hold 1 second at the top, return slowly. ALL reps on one leg, then switch.
💡 Slight forward lean from the hip is fine. Glute should be fully contracted at the top. Don't arch your lower back — if it arches, you've gone too far
Ankle Dorsiflexion Pull Sets1 Reps12 each foot Rest15s
1 REP = Band anchored behind you, looped around the top of your foot. Seated or standing. Pull your toes UP toward your shin as far as possible against the resistance, hold 1 second, slowly return. ALL reps on one foot, then switch.
💡 This is a small movement — feel the front of the shin (tibialis anterior) working. The slow return is as important as the pull. Don't let the band snap your foot back down
💪 Extension kick loads the hip drive behind every forward hop · Dorsiflexion trains foot strike for linear speed

Inside Cut Cone Drill Duration8 reps each side — weak foot first
WHAT = Dribble to a cone, plant your outside foot just past the ball, sharp inside cut at 45°, accelerate 5 yards. Walk back. WEAK FOOT FIRST — complete all reps leading with weak foot before switching
💡 Plant foot goes PAST the ball, not beside it. The cut is at 45° — not straight back
Outside Cut Cone Drill Duration8 reps each side
WHAT = Dribble to cone, chop step, drop your shoulder inside as a fake, cut outside and accelerate 5 yards. Walk back. That's 1 rep
💡 The shoulder drop sells the fake. If you don't drop the shoulder, it's just turning — not a move

Position Work — Winger

Step-Over into Cut Duration8 reps each side
WHAT = Dribble toward a cone (the defender), perform a full step-over, cut to the outside and accelerate 5 yards past. Walk back. That's 1 rep
💡 The step-over has to look convincing — exaggerate it in training so it becomes natural in games. A half-committed step-over fools nobody
Scissors into Acceleration Duration8 reps each side
WHAT = Dribble to cone, perform scissors move, cut inside or outside, accelerate 5 yards. Walk back. That's 1 rep. Vary which direction you cut each rep
💡 Weight shifts to outside foot during the scissors. The first step after is explosive — don't ease into the sprint. The cut direction should be a decision, not a habit
🏃 Part 2 — SAQ · 15 min
⏱ Timing — 3 passes per drill · Walk back after each pass · 20 sec rest before Pass 3 · ~7.5 min ladder

Ladder · 5 drills

1 LATERAL SL Hop — Lateral (L) Passes3 Rest~20s
1 PASS = Stand IN-LINE with the ladder, face LEFT (sideways). Hop on the FORWARD foot (left foot) through each rung, traveling the full length of the ladder
💡 The forward foot does all the work — it's the foot closest to the next rung. Push laterally from the hip. Stay facing sideways throughout
2 LATERAL SL Hop — Lateral (R) Passes3 Rest~20s
1 PASS = Stand IN-LINE with the ladder, face RIGHT (sideways). Hop on the FORWARD foot (right foot) through each rung, full length
💡 Mirror of L — right foot is now the forward foot. Note if one direction feels less stable
3 LATERAL Lateral Hops (L) Passes3 Rest~20s
1 PASS = Stand IN-LINE with the ladder, face LEFT (sideways). Both feet together, hop laterally through each rung traveling left, full length
💡 Face sideways throughout. Both feet land together inside each rung. Drive from the hips, land softly on balls of feet
4 LATERAL Side Shuffle (L) Passes3 Rest~20s
1 PASS = Stand IN-LINE with the ladder, face LEFT (sideways). Lead foot (left) steps into rung, trail foot (right) follows into the SAME rung — both feet must enter each rung before moving on. Shuffle full length traveling left
💡 NEVER cross feet. Stay low in athletic stance. Both feet enter every rung. Drive from hips not knees
5 LATERAL Side Step (L) Passes3 Rest~20s
1 PASS = Stand BESIDE the ladder, face FORWARD. Left foot (CLOSER to ladder) taps IN then OUT of each rung. Right foot (FARTHER) steps forward to progress. Full length moving left
💡 Always face forward — NOT sideways. Closer foot taps in/out, far foot walks forward. Stay tall, no leaning

Resistance Bands

Lateral Band Walk Sets2 Reps10 yds each way Rest20s
1 REP = Band above knees. Athletic stance, feet shoulder-width, slight squat. Step one foot sideways, then bring the other to shoulder-width — never let feet touch. Walk the full distance with constant band tension. That full distance = 1 set.
💡 Hips stay level the whole time — if they dip or sway, the rep doesn't count. Toes forward, knees tracking over toes, don't stand up out of the squat
Hip Abduction Kick Sets2 Reps10 each leg Rest20s
1 REP = Band around working ankle, anchored to a fixed point at your side. Stand on the opposite foot. Drive the banded leg directly OUT to the side as far as possible, hold 1 second at the top, return slowly. ALL reps on one leg, then switch.
💡 Stand tall — don't lean away from the kick. The hip drives the movement, not the knee. Control the return; don't let the band snap your leg back
💪 Band walk + abduction kick both target glute med — the exact muscle powering every lateral drill

🏃 Part 2 — SAQ · 15 min
⏱ Timing — 3 passes per drill · Walk back after each pass · 20 sec rest before Pass 3 · ~7.5 min ladder

Ladder · 5 drills

1 MULTI-DIR Ickey Shuffle Passes3 Rest~20s
1 PASS = 3-step pattern per rung: lead foot IN, trail foot IN, lead foot OUT to side. Alternate which side you exit each rung. Full length
💡 Lean slightly in direction of travel. Consistent 3-count rhythm. Arms drive the direction change
2 MULTI-DIR Carioca Passes3 Rest~20s
1 PASS = Move LATERALLY — crossover step pattern alternating in FRONT then BEHIND, using rungs as spacing guides. Full length each direction (once L, once R)
💡 Full hip rotation every crossover — this is a hip mobility drill as much as footwork. Eyes forward, smooth rhythm
3 MULTI-DIR Criss Cross Passes3 Rest~20s
1 PASS = Per rung: back foot steps IN, rotate hips and take TWO steps OUTSIDE the next rung — one foot each side, landing straddling it. Pattern: in-out-out, in-out-out. Full length
💡 Hip rotation DRIVES this drill — fully rotate through hips. The two outside steps should land you perfectly straddling the next rung
4 MULTI-DIR Straddle Hops Passes3 Rest~20s
1 PASS = Both feet straddle OUTSIDE rung, hop both feet IN together, immediately hop both feet back OUT straddling next rung. Full length
💡 Snap feet in and out quickly, land softly each time, build a consistent rhythm
5 MULTI-DIR Ski Jumps Passes3 Rest~20s
1 PASS = Face FORWARD, standing to the side of the ladder. One foot hops IN to rung 1 while the other foot lands OUTSIDE and BEHIND it. Next hop crosses to the opposite side: other foot IN to rung 2, first foot OUTSIDE and BEHIND. Alternate which foot enters each rung as you travel the full length
💡 Face forward throughout. Outside foot always lands BEHIND the inside foot. You're crossing side-to-side like a skier. Think: in-behind, cross, in-behind, cross

🦶 Weak Foot Focus — 3 min
These 3 exercises use your weak foot ONLY. No dominant foot. This is the most important block in the session for the next 8 weeks.
1-Touch Wall Pass — Weak Foot Only Duration45 sec
WHAT = Face rebounder 3–4 yards away. All touches with weak foot only — no dominant foot at all this set. Firm passes, controlled receive, continuous rhythm
💡 This will feel awkward at first. That is exactly the right feeling — it means you're in the right training zone. The awkwardness disappears over weeks, not days

💡 If the weak foot rep takes twice as long, that's fine — quality over pace. The goal is a clean complete touch sequence with the weak foot from start to finish
BLOCK 0b: BALL WORK — WITH DAD · 5–8 min
🤝 These exercises need a partner. Dad does this block on full session day. They cannot be done solo — save them for this day only.
Back-to-Goal Receive & Turn Duration10 reps each direction — 50% weak foot
WHAT = Stand with back to parent. Parent plays a firm ground pass from behind you. Receive with one touch, turn quickly using inside or outside of foot, drive 5 yards. Return. HALF of all reps must receive and turn with the WEAK FOOT first. That's 1 rep each direction
💡 Check your shoulder BEFORE the ball arrives — decide your turn direction before you touch it. The player who decides early turns clean. For weak foot reps: the awkwardness is the training stimulus
Angled Receive & Lay-Off Duration10 reps each side — 50% weak foot
WHAT = Player stands facing forward. Parent stands a few yards to the side at roughly 45°. Parent plays a firm ground pass across your body. Turn your hips TOWARD the ball — open up to face the parent — receive with the far foot, lay off back to the parent with the near foot in one fluid motion. HALF of all reps: parent stands on the other side so the weak foot receives. That's 1 rep. All reps one side then swap
💡 The hip turn is the whole point — don't receive square-on. Turn to face where the ball came from, receive with the far foot, lay off with the near foot back in the same direction. One fluid motion, not two separate touches
Variable-Distance Pass to Partner Duration1 full set = 3 distances × 3 passes each
WHAT = Parent starts 5 yards away. Player plays 3 passes, then parent takes 3 steps back to ~8 yards, player plays 3 passes, then 3 more steps to ~12 yards, player plays 3 passes. Parent then moves back to 5 yards without warning mid-set — player must read the new distance and adjust immediately
💡 Don't look at your foot — look at where your parent is. The calibration happens in your brain before your foot contacts the ball. Feel the difference between 5, 8, and 12 yards and make the adjustment automatic. This is what pass weight calibration feels like
Ball Out of the Air Duration8 reps each side
WHAT = Weeks 1–3 — Self-toss: Toss ball to yourself from hands to hip height. Cushion with thigh, let drop to foot, control cleanly to a stop. 8 reps each leg.

Weeks 4–6 — Parent toss, 5 yards: Parent tosses in an arc to chest height. Control with thigh then foot, play back to parent. 8 reps each side.

Weeks 7–8 — Parent toss, moving: Jog to a receiving spot as parent tosses from 8–10 yards. Control in stride, play a directed pass back. Parent calls 'left' or 'right' before the toss to add directional constraint. 6 reps each side.
💡 Don't stab at the ball — get your body behind it early and move toward it. Weeks 1–3 feel easy; that's intentional — you're building the movement pattern before adding difficulty. Follow the progression week by week and don't skip ahead
Wide Run Receive in Stride Duration8 reps each side
WHAT = Make an angled run toward the wide channel. Parent feeds ball ahead of your run into space. Receive in stride while moving at pace, push ball with first touch to continue running, drive to a cone. Walk back. That's 1 rep
💡 Time the run so you're already moving fast when the ball arrives — not accelerating into it after the receive. Your first touch should set up your next stride, not stop you. A winger's best tool is pace in behind — this trains exactly that
Shadow Play — Winger Positioning Duration5 min
WHAT = Silas takes his winger position. Parent calls out ball locations: 'ball at right center back', 'ball at right back', 'ball at #10', 'ball at striker', 'ball switched to left back'. Silas physically moves to where he should be positioned for each scenario. Parent gives immediate feedback: correct, too high, too deep, wrong side. Mix the order and repeat each scenario multiple times
💡 You're not watching the ball — you're scanning and adjusting before it arrives. The habit: when the ball moves, you move. Every time you stand still watching the ball in a game, you're giving a defender time to mark you. This drill trains that habit out of your game

1 PASS = BOTH feet step into rung 1, BOTH into rung 2, BOTH back into rung 1, then progress. Pattern: forward-forward, back-one. Full length
💡 Two steps forward, one step back — both feet each time. Controlled rhythm. Don't rush the backward step
LATERALFace sideways · Travel along ladder length
5 LATERAL Side Shuffle (L) Passes2 Rest~10s
1 PASS = Stand IN-LINE with the ladder, face LEFT (sideways). Lead foot (left) steps into rung, trail foot (right) follows into the SAME rung — both feet must enter each rung before moving on. Shuffle full length traveling left
💡 NEVER cross feet. Stay low in athletic stance. Both feet enter every rung. Drive from hips not knees
6 LATERAL Side Shuffle (R) Passes2 Rest~10s
1 PASS = Stand IN-LINE with the ladder, face RIGHT (sideways). Right foot leads, left foot follows into same rung. Both feet enter every rung. Full length traveling right
💡 Mirror of L. Stay low, never cross feet
7 LATERAL Lateral In & Out (L) Passes2 Rest~10s
1 PASS = Stand IN-LINE with the ladder, face LEFT (sideways). Both feet step IN to each rung together, then both step OUT to the far side together — in-out-in-out — traveling full length to the left
💡 Maintain side-facing position throughout. Quick in-out each rung, stay on balls of feet
8 LATERAL Lateral In & Out (R) Passes2 Rest~10s
1 PASS = Stand IN-LINE with the ladder, face RIGHT (sideways). Both feet in and out of each rung, traveling full length to the right
💡 Mirror of L — face right throughout. Same quick in-out rhythm
9 LATERAL Scissors (L lead) Passes2 Rest~10s
1 PASS = Face SIDEWAYS beside ladder. Split stance: LEFT foot inside rung, right foot outside. Hop and SWITCH — right foot into rung, left foot outside — progress to next rung. LEFT foot leads into rung 1. Full length
💡 Face sideways throughout. One foot always inside, one always outside — they switch every hop. Quick low hops
10 LATERAL Scissors (R lead) Passes2 Rest~10s
1 PASS = Same as Scissors L but RIGHT foot leads into rung 1. Face SIDEWAYS, split stance, hop-switch every rung. Full length
💡 Mirror of L lead — right hip does primary work. Note if one direction feels less coordinated
MULTI-DIRECTIONALHip rotation · Lead-foot switches · Oscillating patterns
11 MULTI-DIR Ickey Shuffle Passes2 Rest~10s
1 PASS = 3-step pattern per rung: lead foot IN, trail foot IN, lead foot OUT to side. Alternate which side you exit each rung. Full length
💡 Lean slightly in direction of travel. Consistent 3-count rhythm. Arms drive the direction change
12 MULTI-DIR Criss Cross Passes2 Rest~10s
1 PASS = Per rung: back foot steps IN, rotate hips and take TWO steps OUTSIDE the next rung — one foot each side, landing straddling it. Pattern: in-out-out, in-out-out. Full length
💡 Hip rotation DRIVES this drill — fully rotate through hips. The two outside steps should land you perfectly straddling the next rung
13 MULTI-DIR Carioca Passes2 Rest~10s
1 PASS = Move LATERALLY — crossover step pattern alternating in FRONT then BEHIND, using rungs as spacing guides. Full length each direction (once L, once R)
💡 Full hip rotation every crossover — this is a hip mobility drill as much as footwork. Eyes forward, smooth rhythm
14 MULTI-DIR Ski Jumps Passes2 Rest~10s
1 PASS = Face FORWARD, standing to the side of the ladder. One foot hops IN to rung 1 while the other foot lands OUTSIDE and BEHIND it. Next hop crosses to the opposite side: other foot IN to rung 2, first foot OUTSIDE and BEHIND. Alternate which foot enters each rung as you travel the full length
💡 Face forward throughout. Outside foot always lands BEHIND the inside foot. You're crossing side-to-side like a skier. Think: in-behind, cross, in-behind, cross
15 MULTI-DIR Straddle In & Out Passes2 Rest~10s
1 PASS = Face forward. Start straddling outside rung 1. Step IN with both feet, then OUT straddling rung 2, IN to rung 2, OUT straddling rung 3. In-out-in-out. Full length
💡 Feet land slightly wider than shoulder-width when straddling. Steady rhythm, don't rush the step-in
BLOCK 2: RESISTANCE BANDS — 8 min
Band above knees for walks, around ankle or waist for sprints.
Lateral Band Walk Sets2 Reps15 yds each way Rest30s
1 REP = Band above knees. Athletic stance, feet shoulder-width, slight squat. Step one foot sideways, then bring the other to shoulder-width — never let feet touch. Walk the full distance with constant band tension. That full distance = 1 set.
💡 Hips stay level the whole time — if they dip or sway, the rep doesn't count. Toes forward, knees tracking over toes, don't stand up out of the squat
Hip Abduction Kick Sets2 Reps12 each leg Rest30s
1 REP = Band around working ankle, anchored to a fixed point at your side. Stand on the opposite foot. Drive the banded leg directly OUT to the side as far as possible, hold 1 second at the top, return slowly. ALL reps on one leg, then switch.
💡 Stand tall — don't lean away from the kick. The hip drives the movement, not the knee. Control the return; don't let the band snap your leg back
Hip Extension Kick Sets2 Reps12 each leg Rest30s
1 REP = Band around working ankle, anchored in front of you. Stand on the opposite foot. Drive the banded leg straight BACK as far as possible, hold 1 second at the top, return slowly. ALL reps on one leg, then switch.
💡 Slight forward lean from the hip is fine. Glute should be fully contracted at the top. Don't arch your lower back — if it arches, you've gone too far
Resisted Sprint Sets4 Reps10 yards per rep Rest90s
1 REP = Band around waist, parent holds the other end behind you. Sprint 10 yards forward against the resistance. Walk fully back. That's 1 rep. Full 90 seconds rest between reps.
💡 Lean forward from the hips, not the waist. Drive your arms hard — arms drive legs. You should feel the band pulling you back slightly the whole sprint. Quality over everything: 4 clean reps beats 8 sloppy ones
Resisted Lateral Shuffle Sets2 Reps10 yds each way Rest30s
1 REP = Band around waist or ankles, parent holds the other end to the side. Shuffle sideways the full distance without crossing your feet. Both directions = 1 set.
💡 Stay low in athletic stance. Never cross feet. Drive from the outside hip — the band should feel like it's pulling you back with each shuffle step
BLOCK 3: PLYOMETRICS & SPEED — 12 min
Land SOFTLY on every jump — if your landing is noisy, absorb more through ankles and knees.
Box Jump Sets3 Reps5 jumps Rest60s
1 REP = Jump up onto the box with both feet, landing softly with knees bent. Stand tall at the top. Step back down — do NOT jump down. Reset fully before the next rep.
💡 Land on balls of feet. If the landing is loud, absorb more through ankles and knees. Never jump back off the box — stepping down protects your knees. Full reset between reps
Lateral Bound (stick landing) Sets3 Reps5 each side Rest60s
1 REP = Stand on one foot. Push off sideways as far as possible, landing on the opposite foot. HOLD the landing for 1 full second with control before the next bound. Each landing = 1 rep.
💡 The hold is non-negotiable — it trains the ankle stability you use every time you cut in a game. If you can't hold the landing, jump a shorter distance. Build distance over weeks, not days
Broad Jump + Stick Sets2 Reps5 jumps Rest45s
1 REP = Two-foot takeoff. Swing arms and jump FORWARD as far as possible. Land on both feet and HOLD for 1 full second without wobbling or taking a step. Reset completely. That's 1 rep.
💡 Land on balls of feet with knees bent — absorb, don't thud. If you stumble forward on landing, you're not controlling it. Stick it: the hold is the whole point
Reaction Sprint Sets6 Reps10 yards per rep Rest60s
1 REP = Start in athletic stance facing your parent. React the instant they give a signal (voice, pointing, held fingers) and sprint 10 yards in that direction. Walk back. That's 1 rep.
💡 Don't anticipate — wait for the signal. The first step is what this entire drill is training. Explode out of your stance like you're reacting to a defender making a move
Flying 10s Sets4 Reps10 yd jog + 10 yd sprint Rest90s
1 REP = Jog the first 10 yards to build speed. The instant you hit the 10-yard mark, explode into an all-out max sprint for 10 more yards. Walk fully back. That's 1 rep.
💡 The transition at the line is the whole point — hit it and go. Don't ease into the sprint. Drive your arms hard and lean into it. This trains top-end acceleration, not just speed
BLOCK 4: STRENGTH — 5 min
Controlled movement only — this is not a race.
Single-Leg Squat to Chair Sets2 Reps8 each leg Rest30s
1 REP = Stand on ONE foot in front of a chair or box. Lower slowly to the seat over 3 full seconds — touch lightly, don't sit. Stand back up. ALL reps on one leg, then switch.
💡 Keep your knee tracking over your toes — don't let it cave inward. Arms out in front for balance. The 3-second lowering is where the strength is built — don't drop fast
Nordic Hamstring Curl Sets2 Reps6 reps Rest45s
1 REP = Kneel on a soft surface, parent holds both ankles firmly down. Lower your body forward as SLOWLY as possible (aim 3–5 seconds), using your hamstrings. Catch with your hands before hitting the ground. Use hands to push back up. That full lowering = 1 rep.
💡 As slow as possible on the way down — that is where the hamstring work happens. It's okay to use your hands on the way up. This is one of the single best exercises for preventing hamstring injuries in soccer
Wall Sit Hold Sets2 Reps30 sec hold Rest30s
1 REP = Back flat against wall, thighs parallel to the floor, feet flat. Hold for the full time. If you need a break, rest 10 seconds and finish the remaining time. The full duration = 1 set.
💡 Feet directly under your knees — not out in front. Don't let knees drift inward. Breathe normally. If your quads start burning, that's exactly what's supposed to happen
BLOCK 5: BALANCE & STABILIZATION — 5 min
Never skip this block — injury prevention, not optional.
Single-Leg Balance (eyes open) Sets1 Reps30 sec each leg Rest15s
1 REP = Stand on ONE foot. Start the timer when the other foot lifts. Hold without touching down for the full time. If you touch down, pause the timer.
💡 Fix your eyes on a single point — this helps significantly. Small ankle adjustments are normal and good — that's your stabilizers firing. Stiffening up makes it harder, not easier
Single-Leg Balance (eyes closed) Sets1 Reps25 sec each leg Rest15s
1 REP = Same as eyes open — stand on ONE foot, start the timer when the other foot lifts. Eyes closed the entire hold. If you touch down, pause the timer.
💡 Much harder without visual feedback — your ankle has to work alone. This is exactly what happens when you land from a jump in a game. Start shorter if needed and build up each week
Single-Leg on Pillow / Towel Sets1 Reps30 sec each leg Rest15s
1 REP = Fold a bath towel into quarters (or use a couch cushion). Stand on it on ONE foot. Hold for the full time on the unstable surface.
💡 The instability forces your ankle stabilizers to fire constantly — this is exactly the training transfer you want for uneven field surfaces and landing from aerial challenges. Eyes open first; close them when you can hold 20 sec
Ankle Dorsiflexion Pull Sets1 Reps12 each foot Rest15s
1 REP = Band anchored behind you, looped around the top of your foot. Seated or standing. Pull your toes UP toward your shin as far as possible against the resistance, hold 1 second, slowly return. ALL reps on one foot, then switch.
💡 This is a small movement — feel the front of the shin (tibialis anterior) working. The slow return is as important as the pull. Don't let the band snap your foot back down

8-Week Phases

Tryouts are approximately 8 weeks away. The plan is structured in 5 phases to peak at the right time — not just train consistently.

📋 How to use this tab: Each week, check which phase you're in and follow its specific instructions. The plan changes across the 8 weeks — a flat 8-week block produces worse results than a periodized one.
Phase 1 — Pattern Building Weeks 1–2

Establish baselines. Learn all exercises correctly. Form before speed — always.

Ball Work Follow plan as written. Weak foot first on all rebounder exercises. Variable-distance passing: learn the feel of 5 vs 8 yards
Ladder Correct mechanics only — don't rush. Eyes up, arms moving, balls of feet
Athletic Follow plan as written. Land every jump cleanly — if landing is messy, reduce intensity
Partner Weeks 1–3 aerial progression — self-toss only. Work on angled receive mechanics
⏱ Time your full ladder circuit at the END of Week 1. Write it down — this is your baseline, not a performance target. Do not try to go fast yet.
Phase 2 — Peak Loading Weeks 3–5

Push for maximum quality on every rep. This is where the adaptation happens.

Ball Work Push pass weight drills harder — vary distances more aggressively. Demand clean first touches on every rep. Increase weak foot reps if they feel more comfortable
Ladder Actively try to beat your Week 1 baseline time. Full explosive intent each rep — arms driving, quick ground contact
Athletic Maximum effort on all sprint and plyometric work. Full explosive intent on every take-off and sprint start
Partner Weeks 4–6 aerial progression — partner toss from 5 yards. Push partner block quality to its highest level
⏱ Re-test full ladder circuit at END of Week 4. Compare to Week 1 baseline. You should see a meaningful drop in time.
Phase 3 — Volume Reduction Week 6

Maintain intensity but begin cutting volume. Protect your body for the final push.

Ball Work Full ball work as normal. Maintain weak foot emphasis. Variable passing at full intensity
Ladder Full session: Silas reduce to 12 drills, Nora reduce to 15 drills. Pre-practice unchanged at 5 drills
Athletic Drop 1 set from each plyometric exercise. Sprint quality and intent unchanged
Partner Full partner block as normal
⏱ Re-test full ladder circuit at END of Week 6. Three data points now — baseline, Week 4, Week 6.
Phase 4 — Deload Week 7

Let the body absorb the training. Reduce volume deliberately — more is not better this week.

Ball Work Full ball work as normal — technical sharpness must be preserved. This is NOT reduced
Ladder Full session: 4 drills, 3 passes, 70% effort — same as Friday session. Pre-practice: reduce to 3 drills, 3 passes
Athletic 1 set only of each plyometric exercise. NO strength work this week at all
Partner Weeks 7–8 aerial progression — partner toss while moving. Full partner block otherwise
Reduce pre-practice from 30 min to 20 min across all 3 days. Skip the optional Friday session entirely this week.
Phase 5 — Competition Taper Week 8 — Tryout Week

Stay sharp and confident. The training is done. Trust it.

Ball Work Ball work and partner block ONLY — this is the full session for this week. Keep quality high
Ladder Pre-practice: 3 drills, 2 passes, 70% effort. No full session ladder this week
Athletic NO plyometrics. NO strength work. None. Do not add any extra work before tryouts
Partner Full partner block — aerial control and pressure work. This is where feel and confidence comes from
The week before tryouts is about feeling good and sharp — not grinding. If you feel tired, rest. If you feel sharp, keep sessions short. The work was done in Weeks 3–5.
