The 5 Gaps This Plan Targets
Every session traces back to one of these five areas.
1 · Top-End Speed — Acceleration mechanics, hip drive, arm action. The first 5–10 yards is the most game-impactful speed type for your position.
2 · Ball Mastery — Close control moves under pressure, first touch to advantage, 1v1 skill sequences. Technical ceiling raises your ceiling in every other area.
3 · Foot Speed + Footwork — Lateral agility, change-of-direction mechanics, quick feet in tight spaces. Different from top-end speed — this is what beats defenders in 2 yards.
4 · Strength On + Off Ball — Shielding posture, low center of gravity, physical duels. Bodyweight only at 13 — body awareness and position, not raw power.
5 · Weak Foot — Passes, shots, and first touch with your non-dominant foot. Consistent dedicated reps compound fast. Most players never do this — it's the fastest route to differentiation at your level.

Friday Session

45–60 min · Open field · No game today, no practice before this, full weekend to recover. This is the primary development window of the week. Full session: warm-up → ball mastery → weak foot → speed + footwork → strength → cool-down.

✅ Protect this session. Friday is the ideal day — legs recovered from Thursday's game, full time ahead. If a conflict comes up, this is the last thing to skip, not the first.
🏃 Dynamic Warm-Up · 5 min Every Friday — before any ball work
Jog + High Knees + Butt Kicks Duration2 min
WHAT = Easy jog 20 yds. High knees back (drive knee to hip height, quick arms). Butt kicks there (heels flick to glutes). Repeat once. Gradual increase in effort — activation, not conditioning.
💡 Quick rhythmic reps wake up the fast-twitch system before you ask it to perform. Don't skip this even if you feel warm.
A-Skips + B-Skips Duration2 lengths each
WHAT = A-Skip: march/skip with high knee drive to 90°, push ball of opposite foot into ground. B-Skip: A-skip but extend lower leg forward at peak of knee drive, then punch it down. 2 lengths each (~20 yds).
💡 A-skip trains knee drive. B-skip trains the pawback. Both are sprint mechanics done slowly so you can feel them — they directly carry over to the acceleration block later in this session.
Hip Flexor Lunge Walk + Rotation Duration1 min
WHAT = Walking lunges — step forward into a deep lunge, hold 1 sec at the bottom, rotate torso toward your front knee, stand up, step forward with the other foot. 10 yds forward and back. The rotation is the key component.
💡 Opens hip flexors of the trailing leg — the push-off leg for sprints. Tight hip flexors are the most common limiter of acceleration range at your age.
⚽ Ball Mastery Circuit · 10 min Both feet — quality first, speed second
📊 Never shorten this block. If time is short on a given Friday, trim the speed block — not this one. Technical ceiling is the hardest thing to build and the most impactful at 13.
Sole Roll Figure-8 Duration2 min
WHAT = 2 cones 3 yds apart. Sole-roll ball in a figure-8 around both cones — alternate feet on each half of the loop. Continuous, fluid, increasing speed. After the first minute: look up while you do it.
💡 Shielding skill + first-touch skill. Eyes up after minute 1 is non-negotiable — ball mastery that only works while staring at the ball isn't game-ready.
Inside-Outside Chop Zigzag Duration2 min
WHAT = 4 cones 2 yds apart in a line. Zigzag through — inside chop at one cone, outside chop at the next, alternating. Both feet taking turns. Last 30 seconds: over-emphasize the outside chop only.
💡 The outside chop creates the most space in 1v1 situations because defenders don't expect it. Over-emphasize it every session in the last 30 seconds.
L-Turn Combination Duration10 reps each foot
WHAT = Approach cone at jog pace. Plant inside foot beside ball. Sole of other foot stops ball. Drag back. Push 90° with inside of plant foot. Burst 3 yards. 10 reps per foot, alternate exit direction each rep.
💡 Every rep ends with a 3-yard burst — not a jog, an actual acceleration. The burst is the payoff of the turn. The move only works in games when followed by speed.
Cruyff Turn Duration8 reps each foot
WHAT = Approach cone as if about to cross or shoot. Wind up the action (fake). Drag ball behind standing foot with inside of shooting foot. Pivot 180°. Push into space with other foot. Accelerate. 8 reps each foot as the dragging foot.
💡 Exaggerate the fake every single rep — lean into the cross motion before dragging. No convincing fake = no space from the turn. Build the habit of selling the fake even in solo practice.
Scissors / Step-Over Duration8 reps each foot
WHAT = Approach cone at jog. Step one foot over the ball in a circular arc (inside to outside). Push ball away with outside of the other foot and accelerate. 8 reps leading with each foot. Once clean: add a double-scissors before the push.
💡 The step-over works through weight commitment — if your body doesn't shift toward the fake foot, the move doesn't sell. Commit your weight before pushing away.
🦶 Weak Foot Development · 10 min Non-dominant foot only — all 10 minutes
📐 Ten dedicated weak foot minutes every Friday. Most players at your level use their dominant foot 95% of the time. Consistent weekly reps compound — but only if you resist switching to your strong foot when it gets difficult.
Wall Pass — Weak Foot Only Duration3 min
WHAT = 4 yds from a wall or rebounder. Continuous weak foot passes — control the return, reset, pass again. Minute 1: aim at the wall. Minute 2: hit a target spot. Minute 3: increase pace, one-touch off the return if possible.
💡 Ankle locked and toe down — same mechanics as your strong foot but your brain works harder to send the signal. The first 20 passes feel wrong. By rep 40 it clicks. Each week the click comes sooner.
Receive + Redirect — Weak Foot First Touch Duration20 reps
WHAT = Roll ball gently 2 yds ahead. Control with weak foot inside — redirect 45° into space. Push 1 yd that direction, jog to catch up, repeat in the other direction. 20 total. Progress to receiving from a wall rebound in Week 3.
💡 First touch puts the ball where you want to go next, not just in front of you. The 45° redirect builds this habit. With your strong foot it's automatic. With your weak foot you have to think — and that thinking eventually becomes automatic too.
Weak Foot Drive — Distance Strikes Duration10 strikes · record ratio
WHAT = Ball stationary 10 yds from wall or target cone. Gentle approach angle. Drive with laces, weak foot only. 10 strikes. Count "clean" contacts (straight line, pace) vs "mishits" (side-spin, scoop, veer). Record clean/10 every Friday.
💡 Track the ratio every week. Watching it climb from 4/10 to 8/10 over 8 weeks is the best motivation you'll find. A clean strike means tight ankle, firm foot, contact just below ball center.
Weak Foot L-Turn + Push Duration8 reps
WHAT = L-turn mechanics — but now your weak foot is the dragging and pushing foot. Approach cone, sole-drag back with weak foot, push 90° with weak foot inside surface, burst away. 8 reps, alternating exit direction.
💡 When you can execute the L-turn with either foot, you can receive and turn in any direction regardless of which foot the ball arrives on. That's genuine two-footedness in a game-realistic sense.
⚡ Speed + Footwork · 15 min Agility timed every Friday · Acceleration mechanics
⚡ Record T-drill and Pro Agility every Friday. These are your primary speed benchmarks — improvement over 8 weeks will be clear and measurable. Full rest between every sprint rep.
T-Drill ⏱ Duration5 timed reps
WHAT = Start cone, top cone 10 yds ahead, side cones 5 yds each side. Sprint 10 yds, shuffle left 5 yds (touch), shuffle right 10 yds (touch), shuffle left to center, backpedal 10 yds to start. Time each rep. 5 reps, 30 sec rest. Record best.
💡 Baseline 13yr soccer: 11–12 sec. Under 10.5 is excellent. 8-week goal: drop 0.5–1 sec from your baseline.
5-10-5 Pro Agility ⏱ Duration5 timed reps
WHAT = 3 cones 5 yds apart. Start center. Sprint 5 yds right (touch), sprint 10 yds left (touch), sprint 5 yds back through center. 5 reps, alternate starting direction, 30 sec rest. Record best.
💡 Drive LOW into every cut — hips drop, not rise. A good cut sounds sharp on turf, not a skid. That's the mechanics difference between a trained and untrained change of direction.
Falling Starts Duration6 × 10 yds
WHAT = Stand tall, feet together. Let your body fall forward past the tipping point. Catch with explosive first step. Drive arms hard from rep 1. Sprint 10 yds full effort. Walk back. 6 reps, 30 sec rest each.
💡 The fall forces correct forward lean before your foot leaves the ground. Most players start too upright. Alternate which foot catches the fall each rep.
Reaction Starts — Visual Cue Duration6 × 10 yds
WHAT = Stand in athletic position. Drop phone or ball from waist height — sprint 10 yds the instant it hits the ground. Vary starting position each rep: facing forward, facing sideways, facing away from sprint direction. 6 reps, 20 sec rest.
💡 Facing-away reps are hardest and most game-realistic. Most sprints in a real game start from a non-ideal body position — pivot, open hips, accelerate. This is the specific reaction you're training.
Quick Feet Cone Touch ⏱ Duration3 × 10 sec · count
WHAT = 5 cones 1 yd apart. Alternate toe-touches as fast as possible — left on 1, right on 2, etc. 10 sec flat-out, 15 sec rest. 3 sets. Count touches per set. Record best.
💡 Stay on toes the entire time. Heels touching = you've slowed down. Arms drive to match feet — this is often the cue that unlocks a faster set.
Box Drill — With Ball Duration3 each direction
WHAT = 4 cones in a 5-yd square. Sprint to top-left, shuffle to top-right, backpedal to bottom-right, shuffle to start — ball at feet through every movement. 3 clockwise, 3 counter-clockwise.
💡 Tight control = faster movement. Sloppy touches slow you down — this is the drill that transfers most directly to close-quarter game situations under ball pressure.
💪 Strength + Shielding · 5 min Body position · Low CoG · On-ball physicality
🛡️ At 13, strength training means body awareness — not weight rooms. These exercises teach the posture and position that make you hard to dispossess. More coachable than raw power and pays off immediately in games.
Shield Stance Holds Duration5 × 10 sec
WHAT = Ball pinned under sole of foot. Wide low stance: feet shoulder-width+, knees ~90°, chest forward, arms out. Hold 10 sec. 5 holds, alternate feet. Feel even weight distribution across both feet.
💡 This is the position you hold with a defender pressing from behind. If you're falling easily, your weight is too high — drop your hips lower.
Push-Up Max Reps Duration2 × to failure
WHAT = Standard push-ups to failure — chest to ground, body straight, elbows 45° from body. 2 sets. Record max reps every Friday. Rest 30 sec between sets.
💡 Track this number weekly. Upper body strength is what wins shoulder duels. Every 5 extra push-ups = noticeably harder shoulder barge in games.
Single-Leg Squat Hold Duration5 × 10 sec each leg
WHAT = Stand on one leg, lower into a quarter squat. Hold 10 sec, knee tracking over toes (not caving inward). 5 holds each leg.
💡 Sprinting and cutting are single-leg movements. If knee caves inward, your hip abductors are weak — and that shows up as wobbling after cuts and increased injury risk.
🌬️ Cool-Down · 5 min Every Friday — especially after speed work
Light Jog + Walking Duration2 min
WHAT = Easy jog 1 min, walk 1 min. Heart rate down, breathing relaxed before stretching.
💡 Sprint work creates lactate. An easy 2-min cool-down clears it faster and reduces next-day soreness going into the weekend.
Hip Flexor + Hamstring + Quad Duration3 min
WHAT = Hip flexor lunge (30 sec each side). Hamstring stretch — foot on cone, lean forward (20 sec each side). Standing quad pull (20 sec each side). Seated groin stretch (30 sec). Ankle circles 10 each foot.
💡 Sprint work heavily loads hip flexors and hamstrings — the two most common strain sites for attackers. 3 minutes here is the minimum investment to keep you available for the weekend session.

Weekend Session

~45–60 min · Open field · Opportunistic — syncs with Eli's session at the same field. First 25 min are parallel independent blocks. Last 20–25 min are a shared partner block training both players simultaneously.

⚡ Structure: Sprint Development (15 min, parallel with Eli's goal kicks) → Weak Foot Shooting (10 min, parallel with Eli's distribution) → Eli + Isaiah Partner Block (20–25 min, shared) → Cool-down.
🤝 Timing: While Isaiah runs sprints, Eli does his goal kick block — both need open field, both work independently. While Isaiah shoots weak foot, Eli does distribution. Then they come together. Neither waits for the other.
⚡ Block 1 — Sprint Development · 15 min Parallel with Eli's Goal Kick Block
📊 Full rest between every rep. Quality at 95–100% beats volume at 80%.
Falling Start Accelerations Duration5 × 10 yds
WHAT = Fall-and-catch start, arms drive, 10 yds full effort. Full walk-back rest. 5 reps to open the session. Reps 4–5 should feel faster than rep 1 as you warm up.
💡 If later reps feel worse, rest periods are too short. Resist the urge to rush through them.
Acceleration Builds — 20 yds Duration4 × 20 yds
WHAT = First 7 yds = 60%, next 7 yds = 85%, last 6 yds = 100%. Gradual ramp trains your ability to keep accelerating past the initial burst. Full walk rest between reps.
💡 Goal: still accelerating at yard 20, not just holding speed from yard 10. In a game, that's the difference between closing a gap and opening one further.
Flying 10s ⏱ Duration3 timed reps
WHAT = 10-yd jog run-up to reach full stride. Sprint absolute hardest for the next 10 yds. Time only the flying segment. Full rest. 3 timed reps. Record fastest.
💡 True top-end speed measurement — you're already at full stride when the clock starts. Track this every weekend. It's your clearest speed benchmark.
Ball Sprint Integration Duration4 reps
WHAT = Dribble 70% for 10 yds. Push ball 3 yds ahead (knock it past a defender). Burst 100% to recover. Control. Jog back. 2 reps dominant foot push, 2 reps weak foot push.
💡 The weak foot push reps are the key ones. When that's reliable, you become genuinely unpredictable — no defender knows which way the knock is coming.
🦶 Block 2 — Weak Foot Shooting · 10 min Parallel with Eli's Distribution Block · At real goal
📐 The only time all week shooting at a real goal with your weak foot. Friday builds the mechanics. This block is the product. Aim for specific spots — not just "at goal."
Stationary Weak Foot Drives Duration10 shots · 12 yds · record on-target
WHAT = Ball stationary 12 yds from goal. 30° approach angle. Drive into bottom corners — 5 shots bottom-left, 5 shots bottom-right. Count on-target. Record every weekend session.
💡 Bottom corners are hardest for keepers to save. Aim there, not at the middle. The on-target count is your weekly weak foot shooting data point.
Inside-Foot Placement — Weak Foot Duration10 shots · 10 yds
WHAT = Inside surface of weak foot only. 5 shots near post, 5 far post. 10 yds out. Accuracy over power — completely.
💡 A well-placed side-foot to the far post at 10 yds is harder to save than a powerful mishit anywhere else. Build accuracy first.
Moving Weak Foot Finish Duration10 shots
WHAT = Dribble from 20 yds, cut to weak foot side, shoot on the move. Shoot from wherever you arrive — don't wait for a perfect setup. 10 reps, different approach angle each time.
💡 Most game-realistic drill in the block. Game finishes happen from irregular positions. If quality drops significantly on the move vs stationary, that's your primary gap.

Both players training simultaneously. Roles listed per exercise.

🤝 Block 3 — Eli + Isaiah · 20–25 min Both players training simultaneously

Isaiah Serves → Eli Extension Dives Isaiah ServesEli Saves Duration6 each side
WHAT = Isaiah stands 8–10 yds from Eli and rolls or underarm-throws balls just beyond Eli's collapse range. Eli executes power-step extension dives. Isaiah varies height and pace each serve (rolling, mid-height, shoulder-high) — aim for a specific spot on each serve. 6 each side.
💡 Isaiah: Serve accuracy trains your throw-in precision. Vary height to give Eli a full range of dives. Eli: Watch Isaiah's arm angle for the direction cue, not the ball.
1v1 Full Speed — Isaiah Attacks Isaiah AttacksEli Defends Duration8 reps
WHAT = Isaiah starts 12 yds from goal with the ball. Eli in goal. Isaiah attacks at game speed. Eli uses his 1v1 framework. Full rest between reps. Isaiah must use a different ball mastery move each rep — no pure-pace runs allowed.
💡 Isaiah: One skill move per rep, no exceptions. Rotate Cruyff, L-turn, outside chop, scissors. Pure speed isn't training. Eli: Call your technique name before executing.
Eli Rolls → Isaiah Weak Foot Finish Eli RollsIsaiah Shoots Duration10 reps × 2 sets
WHAT = Set A: Eli rolls to Isaiah's weak foot side from goal (15 yds out). Isaiah controls on weak foot, one touch, shoots weak foot only. 10 reps. Set B: Eli rolls to strong side — Isaiah must transfer and finish with weak foot. 10 reps.
💡 Isaiah: Set B (strong-side receive, weak-foot finish) simulates a cross from the "wrong" side — finish to the far post. Hardest and most valuable weak foot drill in the plan. Eli: Vary roll pace to make Isaiah adjust control.
Eli Punts → Isaiah First Touch + Turn Eli ServesIsaiah Controls Duration8 reps
WHAT = Eli punts or throws high ball for Isaiah 15–20 yds out. Isaiah controls (chest, thigh, or foot) directing it 2–3 yds into space. Second touch: drive and shoot. 8 reps, Eli varies height slightly each rep.
💡 Isaiah: First touch dead at your feet lets a defender close. First touch into space keeps you free. Eli: This is your punt maintenance work — same quality you'd use in a game. Isaiah's first touch is your placement accuracy test.
🟣 Solo Finishing Block · 20 min Use when Eli is unavailable
🟣 Solo option — swap in for the Eli Partner Block when training alone after sprint and weak foot blocks.
Combination Finishing — Self Serve Duration10 reps varied
WHAT = Roll ball 5 yds to yourself, sprint onto it, one touch to set, finish. Vary: driven, placed inside, weak foot, outside foot, on the move. Name the finish type before each rep.
💡 Deliberate variety beats random repetition. Naming the finish type before each rep makes it intentional.
Skill Move + Finish Sequence Duration10 reps
WHAT = Start 25 yds from goal. Dribble, execute one ball mastery move at a cone "defender" 12 yds out, finish. Different move each rep — Cruyff, L-turn, inside chop, outside chop, scissors. Never same move twice in a row.
💡 Commit to the move as if the cone is real. A half-committed Cruyff against a cone becomes a half-committed Cruyff in a game.
Extra Weak Foot Shots Duration10 shots
WHAT = 10 additional weak foot shots — 5 from 10 yds, 5 from 15 yds. Solo days get extra weak foot volume in place of the partner block.
💡 Solo days are actually excellent weak foot volume days. Use the extra time intentionally.

8-Week Training Structure

No imminent tryout for Isaiah — the 8 weeks is a focused development block. Phase 4 is a forward maintenance plan, not a taper. The 8-week template is designed to repeat.

Phase 1 — FoundationWeeks 1–3

Build technical habits before adding intensity. Learn what correct feels like — falling-forward in sprints, ankle lock in weak foot strikes, eyes-up in ball mastery. Establish baselines.

FridayWeek 1: RPE 5–6. Ball mastery at controlled speed — technique first, pace later. Speed block: falling starts and box drill only, skip reaction starts. Week 3: all blocks at full structure. 45 min max until Week 2.
Weak FootWall passes and stationary shots only. Count clean contacts vs mishits every Friday and weekend. Measure honestly — you need a real starting number.
WeekendFalling starts and 20-yd builds only — no flying 10s yet. Eli partner block at 70% speed (introduction, not competition). Verbalize what GK technique Eli is using so Isaiah can practice reading it.
Baseline TestEnd of Week 1 (Friday or weekend): T-test, pro agility, flying 10, broad jump, weak foot clean-contact ratio. Starting numbers — not judgments, measurements.
Week 1 should feel manageable. Build the habit of showing up on Friday before building the intensity of the session.
Phase 2 — DevelopmentWeeks 4–6

Push intensity, add complexity. Reaction starts in. Flying 10s timed. 1v1s go full speed. Weak foot shifts from mechanics to application. Peak load weeks 5–6.

FridayRPE 7–8 throughout. Full speed block including reaction starts. Ball mastery: add directional constraints — "only L-turns exiting left" — to force weak-side decisions. T-test and pro agility times should be dropping.
Weak FootShift to moving weak foot work. Weak foot L-turn introduced. In games: target 3 weak foot passes per game, tracked mentally. No pressure — just count.
WeekendIntroduce flying 10s — time and record. Ball sprint integration adds weak foot push reps. Eli 1v1s full speed, skill move required every rep.
Mid-PointEnd of Week 4: re-test T-test, pro agility, flying 10, weak foot ratio. Agility adaptation is the fastest to show — you should see clear movement by now.
Weeks 5–6 are peak load. If Friday leaves heavy legs heading into a game week, trim the speed block that Friday — keep ball mastery and weak foot in full.
Phase 3 — ConsolidationWeeks 7–8

Reduce volume, maintain intensity. Trust the patterns. Apply new skills deliberately in games — count weak foot passes, 1v1 wins, times a ball mastery move worked under real pressure.

FridayCut 1 set from each speed exercise. Maintain full ball mastery and weak foot blocks — these are never shortened. RPE 8 on the reps that remain.
WeekendFlying 10s for final benchmark (Week 7). Full 1v1 intensity. Feel sharp, not depleted.
Final TestEnd of Week 7: T-test, pro agility, flying 10, broad jump, weak foot ratio. Compare to Week 1 — this is the evidence of 8 weeks of work.
Game FocusPick ONE intention per game: "only skill moves to beat defenders — no pure pace" or "every weak foot attempt I complete." Single focus per match compounds faster than general effort.
Weeks 7–8 should feel like the new normal — habits built. Trust them.
Phase 4 — Maintenance + RepeatOngoing · Summer + Next Season

Unlike his siblings' taper, Phase 4 is a forward plan. The 8-week block is a template to repeat. Friday sessions continue through summer.

SummerKeep the Friday habit through summer — park or open field, full session. 2 sessions/week if possible (Friday + one weekend). Partner blocks with Eli or Silas when schedules align. The habit of showing up is the most transferable skill from this program.
Weak FootTarget for next fall (8th grade): 8/10 clean contacts. Weak foot passes in games feel like a choice, not a last resort. Math: 3 weak foot blocks/week × 40 weeks ≈ 12,000 weak foot touches. That's what closes the gap permanently.
Next BlockAfter 2 weeks lighter, reset to Phase 1 with new baselines. Second cycle starts with noticeably better scores than this cycle's Week 1. Each cycle builds on the last.
Varsity Path7th grade now. Varsity typically 10th grade (age 15–16). The players who make it at 16 started doing this quiet work at 13. The majority of his competition will not.
The goal isn't this season — it's having options in 3 years.
